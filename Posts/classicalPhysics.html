Hamiltonian Mechanics

Let $M$ be a $2n$-dimensional manifold and denote the with coordinates $\mathbf{p},\mathbf{q}\in\mathbb{R}$. The 2-form $\omega^2 \equiv d\mathbf{p}\wedge d\mathbf{q}$ is then a natural structure of the manifold. Now assume $\mathbf{p}$ and $\mathbf{q}$ are chosen such that $\omega^2$ is non-degenerate, i.e $\omega^2: T_\mathbf{x}M \times T_\mathbf{x}M \rightarrow C^\infty(M,\mathbb{R})$ is bijective. That is $\omega^2(\mathbf{v},\mathbf{u})=0 \ \forall \mathbf{v} \implies \mathbf{u}=0$. Or, if you prefer, $\omega^2$, which can be considered a function from vector fields to functions on $M$, is an isomorphism. The non-degeneracy of $\omega^2$ mean that one always associate a 1-form $dF \in T^*_\mathbf{x}M$ with a vector field $\mathbf{F}\in T_\mathbf{x}M$ by $dF \equiv \omega^2(\mathbf{F},\cdot)$. Let us denote this map by $S:T^*_\mathbf{x}M\rightarrow T_\mathbf{x}M$. It is nice to notice that $S^{-1} \partial_\mathbf{p} = (d\mathbf{p}\wedge d\mathbf{q})(\partial_\mathbf{p})=-d\mathbf{q}$ and $S^{-1} \partial_\mathbf{q} = d\mathbf{p}$. This means that for any function $H:M\rightarrow \mathbb{R}$, we have $dH = (\partial_\mathbf{p} H)d\mathbf{p} + (\partial_\mathbf{q} H)d\mathbf{q}$ and therefore \[ S dH = (\partial_\mathbf{p} H)\partial_\mathbf{q} - (\partial_\mathbf{q} H)\partial_\mathbf{p} \] Curiously, any flow $\dot{\mathbf{x}}\equiv SdH(\mathbf{x})$ along $SdH$ is then equivalent to the equations \[ \frac{d\mathbf{q}}{dt} = \frac{\partial H}{\partial \mathbf{p}} \text{ and } \frac{d\mathbf{p}}{dt} = -\frac{\partial H}{\partial \mathbf{q}} \]
